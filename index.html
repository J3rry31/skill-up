About Ann-Kio Briggs

Renowned Environmental/Niger Delta Rights Activist and Humanitarian Ann-Kio Briggs is taking a bold step toward empowering young minds by sponsoring a cutting-edge tech program at Rivers State University, Emohua Campus. This initiative is designed to equip students with essential digital skills, fostering innovation and preparing them for the rapidly evolving global tech landscape.

A firm believer in justice and opportunity, Briggs has long been at the forefront of advocating for human rights and sustainable development. Through her AGAPE Birth Rights Organization and unwavering commitment to the Ijaw people, she has championed the cause of equity and empowerment. Her latest initiative in the tech space is a testament to her vision—creating opportunities for young Nigerians to thrive in an increasingly digital world.

Speaking on the program, Briggs emphasized the power of choice in shaping one’s destiny:

"Everybody has the capacity to be good, even the best, so long as you aspire and keep working in that direction. It is all a matter of choice, and the choices we make in life are extremely powerful."

With this program, she is not only providing students with valuable tech skills but also inspiring them to make choices that will lead to a brighter future for themselves and their communities.
